---
title: "12.3 Ownership Of IP And Invention Disclosure "
---

**Company Ownership (Default Rule)**

Unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing:

All intellectual property and work product that is:

- created during working hours, or
- created using Company resources, or
- created as part of Company duties, or
- related to the Company’s business, products, services, or portfolio companies,

shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

**4.2 Work Created Outside Working Hours**

Work created outside working hours, on personal time, or using personal equipment shall still be deemed Company property if it:

- relates to Company or portfolio business,
- arises from knowledge, access, or exposure gained through employment,
- builds upon or derives from Company or portfolio work,

could reasonably be anticipated as part of the Company’s business or roadmap.

**4.3 Waiver of Rights**

To the extent permitted by law, employees:

- irrevocably assign all rights, title, and interest in such IP to the Company,
- waive any moral rights or claims of authorship or ownership,
- agree to execute documents necessary to perfect or enforce Company ownership.

**5. Inventions and Disclosure Obligation**

5.1 Mandatory Disclosure

Employees must promptly disclose to HR:

- any invention, improvement, idea, framework, or concept
- conceived, developed, or reduced to practice during employment

that relates to Company or portfolio activities.

**5.2 Disclosure Format**

Disclosure must be made in writing and include:

- description of the invention or work,
- date of creation,
- collaborators (if any),
- relationship to Company or portfolio work.

Failure to disclose may be treated as misconduct.
